The correlation between PEF-derived predictions and observed information content was $r=0.900$ ($p<0.001$; computed across all \PEFtotalStudies{} KPI studies in the primary sports analysis, not the four illustrative scenarios above). The four scenarios in \cref{tab:scenarios} are representative examples drawn from that larger set to span the quadrant space. The first two scenarios illustrate the efficiency--power tension: $\eta<1$ coexists with positive ML improvement, consistent with the information content analysis in \S2.2.3.

\subsection{Primary Analysis: Sports KPIs}

The sports KPIs span all four quadrants (\cref{fig:pef_landscape}). Rugby yielded mean $\eta=\PEFrugbyMeanEta$ (95\% CI: $[\PEFrugbyCIlo,\PEFrugbyCIhi]$, \PEFrugbySuccess\% success rate across \PEFrugbyNkpis{} KPI studies). Football yielded mean $\eta=\PEFfootMeanEta$ (95\% CI: $[\PEFfootCIlo,\PEFfootCIhi]$, \PEFfootSuccess\% success across \PEFfootNkpis{} KPI studies). The distribution of KPIs across quadrants, and the per-KPI season-to-season stability of $(\kappa,\rho)$ positions, is visualised in \cref{fig:pef_landscape}.

KPIs with positive correlation (e.g., kicks from hand, carries) fell into Quadrants~1--2 and yielded $\eta>1$. KPIs with negative or near-zero correlation (e.g., turnovers won, penalties conceded) fell into Quadrants~3--4 and yielded $\eta\le 1$, yet several still showed positive ML improvement, consistent with the efficiency--power tension described in \S2.2.3.

\subsection{Supporting Validation: Cross-Domain Results}

\Cref{tab:validation} summarises results across all six domains. Supporting domains illustrate how empirical $(\kappa,\rho)$ structures propagate into $\eta$ and success rates (here, proportion of units with $\eta>1$ and $p<0.05$ where hypothesis tests apply):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Healthcare} \parencite{nhanes2017pbxo}: pooled $\eta=\PEFhealthMeanEta$ (bootstrap 95\% CI: $[2.448,2.622]$) across $N=\PEFhealthN$ {NHANES} participants pairing systolic and diastolic blood pressure from the same oscillometric measurement (Quadrant~2). Pulse pressure ($\mathrm{SBP}-\mathrm{DBP}$) is the clinically established relative feature.
  \item \textbf{Clinical genomics} \parencite{brunner2014earlybreast,geoGSE47462}: mean $\eta=\PEFgenomicsMeanEta$ ({SD} $=\PEFgenomicsSdEta$) across $\PEFgenomicsN$ genes ranked by paired-differential signal in {GSE47462}; $\PEFgenomicsSuccess\%$ of genes yielded $\eta>1$.
  \item \textbf{Finance}: mean $\eta=\PEFfinanceMeanEta$ ({SD} $=\PEFfinanceSdEta$) across $\PEFfinanceN$ {S\&P}~100 stocks relativised to daily {S\&P}~500 returns (2020--2023 pinned snapshot); $\PEFfinanceSuccess\%$ success rate.
  \item \textbf{Manufacturing} \parencite{openml752bosch}: mean $\eta=\PEFmfgMeanEta$ ({SD} $=\PEFmfgSdEta$) on {Bosch} {OpenML} 752 {CNC} cycles ($N=\PEFmfgN$ axis-wise pairings). Approximately $\PEFmfgSuccess\%$ of pairings yielded $\eta>1$.
\end{itemize}
Detailed visualisations and per-study breakdowns for these domains are provided in the Supplementary Information.
